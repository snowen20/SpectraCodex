*Spectra Codex* is an independent Fortean publication investigating paranormal, ufological, and allied phenomena through a structural-comparative lens.

The publication operates from a working position rather than a settled one: that the phenomena traditionally grouped as paranormal, occult, ufological, cryptozoological, and supernatural may share a common structural origin — a sophisticated non-human intelligence, or set of intelligences, operating across deep time, possibly tied to the authorship of human biology itself.

This is not a hypothesis we defend. It is a candidate the evidence has not yet eliminated. The investigation proceeds by cataloging structural invariants across independent witness traditions — what remains constant across cultures, centuries, and disciplines — and letting the candidate set of explanations shrink through accumulation rather than assertion.

*Spectra Codex* is an unmasking project, not a hypothesis-testing project.

## Editorial Framework

The investigation works across four axes: **mechanism** (how does it operate), **location** (where is it, in what sense), **ontological class** (what kind of thing is it), and **form constancy** (one operator with many masks, or many operators sharing a method).

Recurring article types include **Framework Features** — deep-dive investigations of specific structural invariants — alongside feature articles, secondary pieces, and an editor's letter in each issue. A standing framework reference page on this site synthesizes findings across issues and updates as the framework develops.

## Intellectual Lineage

The publication draws from the work of John Keel, Jacques Vallée, John Mack, Diana Walsh Pasulka, Jeffrey Kripal, Mac Tonnies, Patrick Harpur, Joshua Cutchin, Greg Bishop, MJ Banias, Nathaniel Gillis, and others engaged in serious structural investigation of high-strangeness phenomena.

## Format

Discrete numbered issues. Each issue contains a feature article, secondary articles, an editor's letter, a framework reference, a bibliography, a glossary, and a what-we're-reading section. Distribution is dual format: PDF download and on-site web reading.
