## The Four Investigative Axes

**Mechanism** — How does it do what it does? What physical, informational, or ontological operations are required to produce the observed effects? What are the constraints on its operation, and what do those constraints imply about its nature?

**Location** — Where is it, in what sense? Does it occupy a position in physical space, in information space, in a substrate below observable matter, or in some relational configuration with respect to the observer? The answer may not be mutually exclusive.

**Ontological class** — What kind of thing is it? Is it a discrete agent, a process, an emergent property of some larger system, or something for which our ontological categories are inadequate? The investigation so far suggests that the inadequacy of categories is itself data.

**Form constancy** — Is there one operator wearing many masks, or many operators sharing a common method? The evidence for each case is substantial. The investigation remains open.

## Current Framework Concepts

The following concepts are under active development. Each will receive a dedicated Framework Feature article in a forthcoming issue.

### The Bound-Lords Hierarchy

A provisional taxonomy of entity classes defined by their apparent operational constraints — what they can and cannot do, what they require, what they avoid. The "bound" designation refers to the consistent pattern across traditions of entities that operate under constraints not of their own choosing.

*Framework Feature article: forthcoming.*

### The Invitation Requirement

The apparent constraint that certain operators cannot cross specific thresholds — physical, psychological, or ontological — without the explicit consent or active invitation of the contacted party. The consistency of this pattern across vampire traditions, angelic accounts, contractual demon typologies, and modern abduction/contact narratives is among the most striking structural invariants the investigation has documented.

*Framework Feature article: forthcoming.*

### The Library of Levers

Working term for the apparent catalog of methods by which the operator induces specific states in the contacted party — fear, awe, amnesia, compliance, ecstatic experience — and the consistency of these methods across traditions. The levers appear to be few, well-understood by the operator, and applied with precision.

*Framework Feature article: forthcoming.*

### The Substrate Floor

Working term for the apparent lower limit of physical interaction available to the operator: the minimum material engagement — blood, sexual contact, ingested substance, physical mark — required to initiate or sustain certain classes of operation. Why the requirement exists is unclear. That it exists consistently is not.

*Framework Feature article: forthcoming.*

### The Replacement Problem

The observation that in a significant subset of contact narratives, the contacted party reports a sense that something has been taken and something has been left in its place — a replacement at the level of identity, memory, or purpose. The relationship between replacement events and the operator's broader operational objectives is a central open question.

*Framework Feature article: forthcoming.*

### The Serpent Anomaly

The serpent is the most persistent morphological element across independent contact traditions — appearing in Genesis, in Gnostic cosmologies, in Mesoamerican accounts, in modern reptilian contact reports, and in the shamanic traditions of every inhabited continent. Whether this consistency reflects a literal morphology, a symbolic convergence, or an operator preference for specific psychological triggers is unresolved.

*Framework Feature article: forthcoming.*
